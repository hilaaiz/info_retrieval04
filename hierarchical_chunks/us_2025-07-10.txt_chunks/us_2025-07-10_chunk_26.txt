Yet, Alan's impact reaches far beyond his professional endeavors.
He is a man rooted in community--his circle of friends from square dancing, church, the fire company, scouting, and his own neighborhood speak volumes about his caring character and connection to others.
He unites the people of this community and strengthens the bonds that make living in close-knit towns so special.
In 1991, Alan married his true love, the late Desiree Bleam.
Together they shared 32 years of love, laughter, and devotion.
Alan stood by her side through her 13-year battle with Scleroderma, providing unwavering support and care, in both his personal and professional endeavors, Alan was a committed man--committed to his family and community.
Today, I commemorate Alan Bleam for his 40 years of remarkable service for our community.
