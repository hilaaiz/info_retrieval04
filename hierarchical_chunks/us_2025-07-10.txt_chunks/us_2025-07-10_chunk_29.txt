Mr. Renz's tenure is marked by numerous accomplishments.
He spearheaded major urban improvement projects, including a $2.4 million streetscape enhancement for Myrtle Avenue and played a pivotal role in helping secure a $55 million upgrade of the Myrtle-Wyckoff transit hub, enhancing accessibility for thousands of daily commuters and consumers.
As an advocate for open space, he successfully championed the conversion of underutilized spaces into public plazas for the community.
Today, the BID corridor is home to four new public plazas enjoyed by all.
As a lifetime resident of Ridgewood, 
Mr. Renz public service extends beyond the BID.
He has served as Executive Director of the Ridgewood Local Development Corporation (RLDC) and as a member of Queens Community Board 5, consistently advocating for small businesses, public safety, and quality-of-life improvements.
His collaboration with city agencies, elected officials, and local stakeholders has left a lasting impact on the Ridgewood community.
