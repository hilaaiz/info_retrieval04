Mr. FITZPATRICK.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize an outstanding constituent from my district, 
Mr. Alan Bleam.
This year, Alan celebrates his 40th year with Plumstead Township.
It is my distinct honor to commend him for four decades of resolute service to our community.
A lifelong resident of Plumstead Township, Alan truly embodies the spirit.
From attending Gayman Elementary to Middle Bucks Area Vocational Technical School, serving as a Junior Firefighter, and achieving the rank of Eagle Scout, Alan has always demonstrated a deep commitment to public service.
In 1982, Alan became an Active Member of the Plumsteadville Volunteer Fire Company.
Just three years later, he took on leadership roles both as Recording Secretary and First Lieutenant.
Over the years, Alan's service with the fire company was marked by a deep sense of duty--a reflection of his strong desire to serve and protect the community he's always called home.
In 1994, Alan assumed the role of Roadmaster for Plumstead Township, eventually rising to become the Director of Public Works--a position he holds with great pride to this day.
