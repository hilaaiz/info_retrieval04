Mr. LEVIN.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize the life of Richard C. Parsons, who passed away on May 16, 2025, in San Clemente, California.
In his memory, I would like to pay tribute and reflect on the life he led and the impact he had on our Nation and the community in California's 49th District.
Richard was born in New York City on August 3, 1934, and was the son He attended Columbia University before earning his degree from Principia College in Elsah, Illinois.
During his time at Principia College, Richard met his wife Nancy, whom he was married to for 66 years.
After attending Principia College, Richard enlisted in the Army.
He served with honor at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium.
After his time serving our nation, Richard settled in Southern California where he led a successful career, retiring as the Vice President of Sales and Marketing for Arrowhead Water.
Richard was an incredible leader and an inspiration to many.
I am grateful for his service to our country.
Richard is survived by his children, grandchildren, and extended family.
They were his greatest pride.
Richard was a dedicated family man who cared deeply for everyone around him.
Richard leaves behind a legacy defined by love and service.
I am proud to have the opportunity to recognize the legacy of Richard C. Parsons.
