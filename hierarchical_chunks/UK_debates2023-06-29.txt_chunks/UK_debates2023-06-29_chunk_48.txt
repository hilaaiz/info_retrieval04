The Governor of the Bank of England earns more than £10,000 a week.
The Bank made billions of pounds in profit from last year’s catastrophic mini-Budget, and the bonuses that are jointly earned by his staff add up to more than £23 million.
How can he use his lofty position to criticise workers who are struggling to get a pay increase, when pay rises have fallen so far behind inflation?
Can we have a debate in Government time to discuss this issue and bust the myth that wages are somehow creating the inflation problem that we have at the moment?
