The work undertaken by the coroner is not a box-ticking exercise or a bureaucratic hurdle.
In the context of assisted dying, it is an extremely powerful deterrent against abuse and malpractice.
Again, to quote Judge Thomas Teague KC, in a letter to The Times on  7 May  this year, he said that the removal of “any realistic prospect of an effective inquest...would magnify, rather than diminish, the obvious risks of deception and undue influence”.
