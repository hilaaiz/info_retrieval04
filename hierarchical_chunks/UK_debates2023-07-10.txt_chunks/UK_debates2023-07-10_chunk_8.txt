I thank the Secretary of State for that answer, but the heads of all 31 UN agencies have called for possession decriminalisation, and more than 30 countries have made changes that have cut deaths and incarceration.
There is no reasonable, rational and evidenced cause for the UK Government, or the Labour party, to reject the proposals out of hand.
May I ask him seriously, in his role as Minister for Intergovernmental Relations, to be  the grown-up on his side of the Chamber, and work and engage with the Scottish Government and drug campaigners on the issue?
