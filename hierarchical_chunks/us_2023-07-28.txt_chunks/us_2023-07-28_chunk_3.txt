Mr. KIM.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor an exceptional member of the Army National Guard, Major Miranda Summers Lowe and her distinguished service and dedication to my office and her work to connect me and my office better with the National Guard.
A native of Muncie, Indiana, Major Summers Lowe enlisted in the Indiana Army National Guard and was later commissioned as an intelligence officer.
She has served 21 years in Indiana, Virginia, Rhode Island, and Washington D.C. National Guards in a broad range of drill status and Active-Duty assignments, including deployments to Iraq in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom, and to Djibouti in support of Operation Enduring Freedom.
Currently, she is excelling as a military personnel liaison for the National Guard Bureau Office of Legislative Liaison, where she is working on ways to improve the quality of life for our National Guard Servicemembers and Airmen.
This work builds off of her years of experience in the Legislative Liaison Division as the Congressional Activities Branch Chief and an Army Congressional Fellow in my office.
Major Summers Lowe has served Active-Duty tours in the Chief of Staff of the Army's Operation Enduring Study Group and the Office of the Director, Army National Guard.
Her attention to detail and responsiveness strengthened the relationship between my office and the National Guard immeasurably.
It's my understanding that Major Summers Lowe formulated the congressional engagement plan and prepared National Guard leadership for over 130 meetings and hearings, as well as playing a pivotal role in informing congressional members about the National Guard Bureau's legislative priorities.
Her efforts played a pivotal role in developing National Guard healthcare access improvements.
