The unduly lenient sentence scheme is intended for use in serious cases for offenders sentenced in the Crown court.
The Attorney General has the power to refer a sentence to the Court of Appeal for review if they believe it is unduly lenient.
A youth court can sentence a child to up to two years of detention only.
Where a child’s offence is likely to attract a sentence of more than two years, the case must be passed to the Crown court for sentencing, where the scheme therefore applies.
