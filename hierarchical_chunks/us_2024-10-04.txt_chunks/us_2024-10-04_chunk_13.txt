Mr. VASQUEZ.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor the memory of Joe Stires and to celebrate his legacy of service.
Joe Stires began his lifetime of service to our community when he joined the United States Air Force, immediately after finishing high school.
He served our community honorably in the Air Force for nearly a decade.
It was during this time that he met his life partner, Matilde.
After leaving the military, Joe took on challenge after challenge.
After working as an insurance agent in Chaparral, Stires eventually opened his own grocery store.
This store became a huge success, leading him to open many more stores.
Throughout his career, Joe never lost his heart for service and giving back to his community.
In his spare time, he became a licensed EMT and eventually a paramedic.
He used these certifications to serve as a firefighter for the Chaparral Volunteer Fire Department for nearly 25 years.
