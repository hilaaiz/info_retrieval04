Mr. GUTHRIE.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to congratulate the Bank of Edmonson County for 100 years of service to its local community.
Founded on October 5, 1925, by brothers Jesse, Luther, and David Alexander alongside business paltners George Woodcock and N.E. (Ed) Vincent, this local financial institution has served the residents of Edmonson County well for a century.
Their foresight during the 1920's, followed quickly by the trials of the Great Depression, laid the foundation for a community-focused institution that has not only endured but thrived.
The Bank of Edmonson County has guided its customers through economic shifts, new technologies, and changing conditions with one main mission in mind: ensuring customers are its highest priority Established with the goal of building a local bank to serve as a pillar of trust and local investment, 100 years later, they continue to focus on supporting Edmonson County and the surrounding area faithfully.
From its humble beginnings with just $15,000 in capital, the bank has grown into a $200 million enterprise, with locations in multiple counties in Kentucky.
