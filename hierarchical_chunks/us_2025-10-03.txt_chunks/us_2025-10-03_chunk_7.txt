Mr. PAPPAS.
Mr. Speaker, I rise to recognize John Danforth, Hooksett Lions Club 2025 Citizen of the Year.
John Danforth exemplifies the community spirit that makes Hooksett and New Hampshire such special places to live, work, and raise a family.
John is dedicated to serving the community of Hooksett, including holding leadership roles in Cub Scout Pack 292, Boy Scout Troop 292, and as a founder of Troop 603.
John has also served on the town's Transfer and Recycling Committee, Budget Committee, Cemetery Commission, and as a Town Councilor.
Additionally, he has held significant positions in the Daniel Webster Council of the Boy Scouts of America, the Hooksett Lions Club, and the Hooksett American Legion, where he helped establish a Gulf War monument at Veterans Park.
A proud U.S. Army veteran, John served during Operation Desert Shield and Desert Storm, and he continues to support veterans as the Acting Veteran Service Center Manager at the Manchester VA Regional Office.
It is thanks to the hard work and dedication of community leaders like John Danforth that the Town of Hooksett and the state of New Hampshire continue to thrive.
On behalf of the consituents of New Hampshire's 1st Congressional District, I congratulate 
Mr. Danforth for being named the Hooksett Lions Club 2025 Citizen of the Year.
I wish him and his family all the best in the years to come as they continue to work to make Hooksett a better place.
