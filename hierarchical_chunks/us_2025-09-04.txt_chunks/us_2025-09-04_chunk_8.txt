Mr. KELLY.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor the service.
Col. Deidre D. Smith, Director of Public Affairs for the Mississippi National Guard.
In this role, she provides critical counsel to the Adjutant General and senior leaders on command information, community outreach, and media engagement, ensuring the Guard remains connected to the citizens.
A Hattiesburg native, Lt. Col. Smith has served across the United States, Europe, and overseas in support of Operation Enduring Freedom and Operation Iraqi Freedom.
Throughout her distinguished career, she has taken on leadership roles in Human Resources, Public Affairs, Information Operations, and Equal Opportunity, demonstrating versatility and excellence in every assignment.
Within the Mississippi Army National Guard, she has held several key positions, including State Director of Public Affairs, Deputy Chief of Staff for Personnel, and Director.
Her dedicated service has earned her numerous awards, including the Meritorious Service Medal, Army Commendation Medal, and the Mississippi Magnolia Cross, reflecting her professionalism and commitment to duty.
Lt. Col. Smith is a graduate of Marion Military Institute, Southeastern Louisiana University, and the University.
She has also completed advanced military education through the Command and General Staff College, the Defense Information School, and the Defense Equal Opportunity Management Institute.
In addition to her military achievements, she was selected for Leadership Mississippi in 2018 and named among the Mississippi TOP 50 in 2022.
She resides in Brandon with her husband, Maj.
James Kevin Polk of the 172nd Airlift Wing, and their daughters, Pfc.
Harley Smith and Pvt.
Lyndi Polk, both newly enlisted members of the Mississippi Army National Guard, continuing their family's proud tradition of service.
