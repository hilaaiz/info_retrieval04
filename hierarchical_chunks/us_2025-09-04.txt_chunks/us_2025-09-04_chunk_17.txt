Mr. Tindall is fondly remembered for his innovative spirit and held a variety of occupations, including a small business owner, a licensed contractor, and a farmer.
He also possessed an array of interests as an avid outdoorsman, tournament fisherman, and horse showman.
He became one of the first to attempt to raise saltwater shrimp in freshwater in southeast Arkansas.
In 2018, 
Mr. Tindall ran for Desha County Judge, and thanks to the broad support of Desha County citizens, he was elected to serve.
After taking office in January 2019, Judge Tindall led Desha County through numerous disasters, flooding events, and the COVID-19 pandemic.
He held this role with great dedication and integrity until the day he passed.
In his role as County Judge, 
Mr. Tindall developed relationships with local, state, and federal agencies to aid county residents through disasters.
He was also responsible for the day-to-day operations of the county, which included management of various departments, oversight of over $5 million in assets, 500 miles of county roads, and an annual budget of over $7 million.
