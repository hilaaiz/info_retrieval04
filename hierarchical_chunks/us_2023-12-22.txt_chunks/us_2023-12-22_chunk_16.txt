Mr. BARR.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor a unique and important business, R.J. Corman Railroad Group, which is headquartered in Nicholasville, Kentucky.
The company is celebrating its 50th anniversary in 2023.
R.J. Corman Railroad Group was founded in 1973 in Central Kentucky by visionary entrepreneur and philanthropist Richard J. Corman with a single backhoe and a dump truck.
The company now operates in 24 states and more than 70 locations throughout our nation.
R.J. Corman Railroad Group employs more than 1,400 people, over 500 of which are based in the state.
R.J. Corman Railroad Group's numerous services encompass railroad construction, short line railroad operations, industrial switching services, transloading operations, emergency response services, track material logistics, distribution centers, signal design and construction, and My Old Kentucky Dinner Train.
The company serves six Class I freight railroads, many regional and short line railroads, as well as various rail-served industries.
R.J. Corman Railroad Company operates three short line railroads serving multiple regions of the state contributing to the economic development of the corresponding communities.
Through its dedicated employees, R.J. Corman Railroad Group strives to be the best-in-class service provider in the industry through its total commitment to customer satisfaction, increasing efficient operations while providing an ever-improving safe, and rewarding work environment.
R.J. Corman Railroad Group is a highly respected Kentucky business.
As the company celebrates its 50th anniversary, I congratulate all their employees and wish them a bright future.
I am honored to recognize this company before the United States Congress.
