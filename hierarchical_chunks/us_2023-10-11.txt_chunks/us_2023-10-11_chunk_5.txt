Mr. ROGERS.
Mr. Speaker Pro Tempore, I rise today in commemoration of the David and Pat Hartline Day.
David is a retired LTC, U.S. Army, best-selling author and past State Commander of the American Legion.
David has invested over 50 years of his life assisting veterans and their families with the problems they face.
He has been nominated for the Presidential Medal of Freedom for his service to this country, its veterans and youth programs.
He been named veteran of the year by the Governor of Alabama and was proclaimed a super citizen by TBS television station.
Pat is a retired registered nurse with over 50 years of service.
She is actively involved with the American Legion Auxiliary.
Pat served with the American Heart Association for a number of years, where she taught principles of heart disease as well as CPR to professionals, students, and communities.
David and Pat have raised four children, ten grandchildren and fourteen great-grandchildren.
They currently reside on the farm that has been in the family since 1840.
The towns of Centre, Cedar Bluff, Leesburg, Gaylesville, and Sand Rock declared Saturday, October 7, 2023 as David and Pat Hartline Day.
