Ms. KAPTUR.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize a milestone year in the Toledo Zoo's remarkable journey in my district.
The Zoo is celebrating 125 years in our community with a series of events culminating in a special gala on Thursday, October 23, 2025.
Rated the top zoo in our Nation, Toledoans are justifiably proud of our treasure.
The Toledo Zoo began modestly in 1900 when Megan Cole donated a woodchuck for display at Walbridge Park along the Maumee River in South Toledo.
The attraction was popular, and within the year, thanks to continued donations, the zoo had 39 animals in its collection.
Unprepared to properly house and care for the growing collection, the Toledo Zoo built the Lion House in 1907.
During these earliest years, when housing was a challenge, animal escapes were not uncommon.
The Toledo Zoological Society was subsequently founded in 1913 to spark development.
