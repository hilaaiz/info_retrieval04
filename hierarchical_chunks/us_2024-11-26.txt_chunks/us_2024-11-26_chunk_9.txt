Mrs. RODGERS.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor a distinguished leader in higher education, President Kirk H. Schulz of Washington State University in Pullman, Washington, who will retire in June of 2025.
President Schulz has dedicated his career to fostering academic excellence, advancing research, and building a campus environment that welcomes all students to succeed.
Today, we recognize and celebrate his legacy and contributions to both the university and the broader community.
President Schulz's tenure at Washington State University is marked by extraordinary achievements.
Under his leadership, Washington State University saw record-setting gains in philanthropy, notably raising $167.9 million in private gifts in fiscal year 2023.
Washington State University received full accreditation for the Elson S. Floyd College of Medicine in 2021 and launched Eastern Washington's first pediatric residency program in 2023.
Throughout his tenure, Washington State University also expanded educational and research facilities across the WSU system, including the U.S. Department of Agriculture's new Plant Sciences Building, Schweitzer Engineering Hall, and a major health sciences remodel on the Spokane campus.
