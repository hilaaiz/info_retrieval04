Mr. BOYLE.
Mr. Speaker, I include in the Record the following Proclamation:
Whereas, Thomas Jefferson University was founded in 1824 as the Jefferson Medical College, now Sidney Kimmel Medical College, and served as one of the nation's first medical schools; Whereas, Jefferson Medical College was the first medical college to open a clinic for the poor and accept free patients; Whereas, Jefferson quickly became a pioneer in medical education, and since its founding 200 years ago, has grown into a renowned university, regional health system, and non-profit health insurer; Whereas, for two centuries, Jefferson faculty and graduates have been innovators and leaders in education, research, discovery and clinical care, with achievements ranging from the discovery of the mosquito vector for yellow fever and the invention of the heart-lung machine to the development of nationally recognized textile engineering and fashion design programs; Whereas today, Thomas Jefferson University is a beacon of success for universities across the nation, with a future-focused model for the education of professionals whose careers will extend through the dynamic 21st century; Whereas, Jefferson has become a research powerhouse whose world-class scholars and investigators work across the spectrum from basic discovery to translation and application of new knowledge; and Whereas, Jefferson has both a growing role in helping solve global society's most pressing problems and a steadfast commitment to improving life for its local communities and people; so now, therefore, be it Resolved, That I congratulate Thomas Jefferson University as it embarks on its third century of education, discovery, healthcare, and community engagement.
