Mr. FROST.
Mr. Speaker, Black History is American History.
The annual observance of Black History Month is more than a chronological account of our past, it is that of our present and our future.
It's about the joy, the culture, the resistance, the fight, and how far Black Americans have come in this country.
Celebrating our Black communities and Black history is so important, especially now, in times like this where values of diversity, equity and inclusion are under attack.
But despite that we move forward in the steps of our ancestors and in the steps of joy and resistance.
