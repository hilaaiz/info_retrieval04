Ms. Manatt has been a leader in public diplomacy.
She currently serves on the board of the Diplomacy Center Foundation, the private partner supporting the completion of the National Museum of American Diplomacy at the U.S. Department of State's headquarters campus.
She was the founding chair of the Council on Women's Leadership at the Meridian International Center, fostering dialogue and leadership development for women in support of Track II diplomacy.
She has always been fascinated by the power of fashion.
She says diplomats defend U.S. interests not in uniforms, but in suits, blouses, ties and, in her case, scarves.
As a member of the GSPM board, 
Ms. Manatt has provided strategic counsel to ensure that students benefited from a strong, bipartisan academic environment and were exposed to diverse career paths.
Through her family's philanthropy, she has also supported other parts of The George Washington University, including the law school, the Graduate School of Education and Human Development, the Elliott School of International Affairs and the Textile Museum.
