Mr. Biden took a swipe at his predecessor's track record last week as he signed a bill extending the program's deadline.
``Many small businesses, as you know, particularly Hispanic as well as African-American small businesses, are just out of business because they got bypassed the first time around,'' the president said.
But 
Mr. Biden's revisions--which, most prominently, expanded lending to independent contractors and others who work for themselves--have run into their own obstacles, including the speed with which they were rushed through.
Lenders, caught off guard, struggled to carry them out, with little time left before the deadline.
``The rules are complicated and constantly changing, and that alone creates an access barrier,'' Randell Leach, the chief executive of Beneficial State Bank in Oakland, Calif.
