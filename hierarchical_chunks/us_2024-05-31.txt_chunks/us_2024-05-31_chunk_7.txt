Mr. Haupt's long career in the military is commendable.
Upon his commissioning during the Cold War, he served as a crewmember on a bomber throughout the 1960s.
During the Vietnam War, he served as a navigator on KC-135 refueling aircraft, spending most of his time doing so stationed in temporary duty in Okinawa.
During this time, he also served as a mission coordinator on EC-47 electronic reconnaissance aircraft.
Our country is safer because of the courage and sacrifice Mr. Haupt has shown throughout his long career.
After retiring from the military in 1984, Mr Haupt was employed as a financial planner, where he worked for about five years.
Later, he taught at the junior high and high school levels for nine more years.
His dedication to serving his community both in and out of uniform is honorable.
Even after his official retirement, 
Mr. Haupt continues to serve his country.
He has conducted admissions outreach for the U.S. Military Academy at West Point for the North Bay Region for thirty years.
He has also served as my Academy Nomination Coordinator, where he has led my nomination panel and helped to nominate dozens of young men and women dedicated to serving our country.
His level of detail to support our nation's future leaders is commendable.
