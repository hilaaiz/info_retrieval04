Mr. DeSAULNIER.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor World War II veteran and D-Day survivor, ``Papa'' Jake Larson, for his invaluable contributions to our nation.
Eager to serve his country, Jake enlisted in the National Guard at just 15 years old after lying about his age.
After the attack on Pearl Harbor in December, 1941, Jake was sent to Fort Dix, New Jersey before heading overseas to Europe.
