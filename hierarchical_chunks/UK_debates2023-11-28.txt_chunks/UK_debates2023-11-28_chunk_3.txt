An estimated 6.3 million households are in fuel poverty across the UK.
Ofgem has announced that energy debt has reached £2.6 billion.
With millions of people facing another difficult winter, the Government promised to consult on a social tariff to help the most vulnerable.
Can the Secretary of State provide an update on that consultation?
People mean many different things by a social tariff, but fundamentally it is about providing people with support to help with their bills.
Just in the autumn statement, we have increased the national living wage, which is worth £1,800 to people; increased benefits by 6.7%, which is worth £470; and cut national insurance contributions, which is worth £450.
Those are all on top of the £900 cost of living support we already have in place.
