Mr. CLYBURN.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor the life and legacy of a great man of faith and a true community servant leader, Deacon Ronald Levester Sewell.
Deacon Sewell, a native of Columbia, South Carolina, passed away on Saturday, May 10, 2025.
Although he is no longer with us in person, his legacy lives on through his life of service.
Deacon Sewell attended C.A. Johnson High School, graduating in 1965.
He furthered his academic journey at Benedict College, earning his degree in 1973.
He later went on to obtain a Master of Education degree from Cambridge College.
Over an extraordinary 35-year career, he transformed countless lives and was a pivotal member of his community.
Deacon Sewell served as an educator, coach, and an athletic administrator within Richland School District One, igniting a passion for learning and excellence in his students.
Beyond his notable contributions in education, Deacon Sewell proudly saved six years in the U.S. Air Force, followed by several years in the Air Force Reserve at McEntire Joint National Guard Base.
His dedicated service included a tour during Operation Desert Storm in the Persian Gulf War, where he exemplified unwavering bravery and dedication to his country.
The morals and values that were the foundation of his life were rooted in his faith in God.
A devoted member of First Calvary Baptist Church, Deacon Sewell's faith was a cornerstone of his life.
He was baptized there and later ordained as a Deacon in 2007 under the leadership.
Reverend Samuel K. Lewis.
Throughout his journey he has been empowered by the encouragement and support of his beautiful family.
Deacon Sewell was married to Maxine Cunningham for 50 years, and was a devoted father to four children:
Dr. Kenya Sewell (Randy) James, Dr. Candace Sewell Hannah, Warren M. Sewell, and Sharon E. Lowman.
