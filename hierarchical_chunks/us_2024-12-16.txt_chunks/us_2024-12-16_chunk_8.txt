Mr. CONNOLLY.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize Grayford F. Payne on the occasion of his retirement from the U.S. Department of the Interior after 38 years of Federal service.
Grayford Payne has spent his career serving the American people in a number of roles within the federal government.
Shortly after graduating from George Mason University in 1980 with a Bachelor of Science in Accounting, 
Mr. Payne served as an Auditor-in-Charge in the Naval Audit Service.
In this role, he was responsible for audit and program reviews programs.
After eight years, Mr, Payne joined the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission where he served seven years as Director of Financial Services.
During his time there, he was in charge of the Commission's financial accounting system and its automated revenue billing and collection system.
In 2002, he joined the Department of the Interior as the Deputy Chief Financial Officer for Indian Affairs where he was responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Office of the Chief Financial Officer which included all financial, accounting, budget, procurement, and property functions.
He was promoted just three years later to the role Assistant Secretary-Management in 2008.
There, he oversaw the planning and execution of the Indian Affairs $4.5 billion annual budget, and his organizational responsibilities encompassed all administrative functions to include finance, budget, acquisition, property management, human resources, homeland security, planning, audit and evaluation, and more.
