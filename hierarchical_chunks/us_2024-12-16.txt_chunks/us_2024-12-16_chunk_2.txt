Mr. CARTER.
Mr. Speaker, I am honored to recognize Dr. Charles Breithaupt for a lifetime of service to Texas education and student athletics.
His active leadership in advancing interscholastic opportunities for young people and his steadfast commitment to student safety exemplify the very best.
Dr. Breithaupt has dedicated over 16 years as the Vice President of the University of Texas at Austin and as the Executive Director of Texas' University Interscholastic League (UIL), supervising academic, athletic, and music competitions at thousands of schools across Texas.
Throughout his tenure, he has made several pioneering achievements, including the implementation of a six-conference alignment, the establishment of the National High School Football Summit and the formation of the UIL Medical Advisory Committee.
The Committee, under his leadership, has developed protocols mitigating health risks for students in high-impact sports.
Dr. Breithaupt's 50-year career exemplifies his passion for student success.
Before working as Executive Director, Dr. Breithaupt served 17 years as a public-school teacher, coach, athletic director, and principal in Southeast Texas.
He currently serves on the Texas Interscholastic League Foundation Board, which awards hundreds of scholarships annually to students attending Texas colleges and universities.
Dr. Breithaupt's numerous accolades include being named 1991 Texas State Coach of the Year, inductions into the Texas Basketball and Texas High School Football Halls of Fame, and two Lone Star Emmy Awards for his role as Fox Sports Southwest High School Spotlight Show Executive Producer, I join Dr. Breithaupt's family, friends, and colleagues in celebrating his unwavering dedication and contributions.
