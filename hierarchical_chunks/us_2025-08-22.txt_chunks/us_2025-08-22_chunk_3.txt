Mr. LaHOOD.
Mr. Speaker, today, I rise to recognize and celebrate the 50th anniversary of the Dirksen Congressional Center.
Located in Pekin, Illinois, the Dirksen Congressional Center has served as a national leader in congressional research, civic education, and the preservation Founded in 1975, the Dirksen Congressional Center began with a mission to honor the legacy of 
Senator Everett McKinley Dirksen by promoting a deeper understanding.
Over the past five decades, it has expanded its reach and influence, serving tens of thousands of students, educators, researchers, and citizens who have benefited from its programs and resources.
The Dirksen Congressional Center has distributed more than $1.4 million in grants to fund groundbreaking research into Congress and its history, trained hundreds of teachers to deliver high-quality civics education through its Congress in the Classroom workshops and produced more than 20 books and monographs about Congress, its leaders, and its operations.
Additionally, the Dirksen Congressional Center's archival collections now preserve the papers of nine Members of Congress, making primary-source documents widely accessible through projects like the American Congress Digital Archives Portal.
