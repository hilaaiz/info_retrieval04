Mr. SOTO.
Mr. Speaker, Chuck Weagraff is a 21-year veteran of the fire service and the proud third generation of his family to wear the badge.
His career has spanned multiple departments and communities, including Honolulu, the cities of Ocoee and Oviedo, and now serving with distinction at the Reedy Creek Fire Department.
At every step, Chuck has demonstrated the highest level of professionalism, dedication, and passion for the calling of a firefighter.
Within the department, Chuck has distinguished himself as both a leader and a teacher.
He serves as an instructor on the Special Operations Team, preparing members for the most complex and dangerous challenges of the profession.
As a paramedic preceptor, he has mentored countless new medics, imparting not only technical skill but also the calm confidence and compassion that define the best in emergency medicine.
In addition, as a certified department fire instructor, he dedicates himself to developing the next generation of firefighters.
His impact as a mentor and instructor is felt throughout our organization and beyond.
Chuck is known among his peers as steady under pressure, generous with his time and knowledge, and always putting the needs of others first.
His quiet but steady leadership inspires those around him to be better, both on and off the job.
Outside the firehouse, Chuck is a devoted family man to his wife and two children.
The same dedication that defines him as a firefighter is equally present in the way he supports and cares for his family.
For his decades of service, professionalism, leadership, and devotion to both his department and his loved ones, Chuck Weagraff exemplifies the very best of the fire service.
