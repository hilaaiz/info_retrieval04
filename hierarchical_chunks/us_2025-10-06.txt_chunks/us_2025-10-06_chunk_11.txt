Ms. MATSUI.
Mr. Speaker, I rise to honor the career and legacy of La Shelle Dozier as she retires from her position as executive director of the Sacramento Housing and Redevelopment Agency (SHRA), where she has served my constituents for over twenty-three years.
I ask all of my colleagues to join me to acknowledge her distinguished career.
Since first joining SHRA in 2002, La Shelle has been integral to the agency's operations and has had a transformative effect on affordable housing opportunities in Sacramento.
Starting as a management analyst, La Shelle quickly learned every facet of the Sacramento housing industry.
She rose very quickly to managing the transformation of South Sacramento's Franklin Villa into the 360-unit Phoenix Park.
She also directed resident services and oversaw the Housing Choice Voucher program that today supports more than 13,000 of my constituents with up to 100 percent of their rent needs.
