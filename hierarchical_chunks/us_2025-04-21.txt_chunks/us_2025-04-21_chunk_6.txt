Mr. KENNEDY.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor and celebrate Dr. Candace Johnson, President and CEO of Roswell Park Comprehensive Cancer Center, for being recognized by the Boy Scouts of America with their prestigious Distinguished Citizen Award.
This recognition is a reflection of her exemplary leadership and unwavering commitment to the values of citizenship, community service, and integrity--values that are central to the Boy Scouts of America and that Dr. Johnson embodies every day As the leader of Roswell Park, Dr. Johnson has dedicated her career to advancing cancer care, promoting research, and improving the lives of countless individuals.
Under her leadership, Roswell Park has become a world-renowned institution, offering state-of-the-art cancer treatment and conducting groundbreaking research that continues to transform the landscape of cancer care.
Her ability to lead with compassion, vision, and determination has inspired a culture of excellence within her organization, one that values both scientific progress and the human dignity of every patient.
Beyond her professional accomplishments, Dr. Johnson has been an advocate for the Western New York community, fostering partnerships and working tirelessly to improve healthcare access and equity.
She exemplifies the Scouting values by being a leader who not only makes a profound impact in her field but also empowers those around her to serve others and contribute to the common good.
Through her work, Dr. Johnson has shown that true leadership means uplifting others, creating opportunities for positive change, and making a lasting difference in the lives of those in need.
