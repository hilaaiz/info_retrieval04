Mr. Thomas Frisbie on his retirement, after an extraordinary career as an award-winning writer and editor.
Since 1976 
Mr. Frisbie has, as he likes to say ``held almost every job at the Chicago Sun-Times besides editor''.
His commitment to bringing Chicago the news has earned many accolades over the years, including the Jacob Scher Award for Excellence in Investigative Reporting for his series covering insurance fraud at Illinois' largest driving school.
He was a finalist for the Peter Lisagor Award twice for his work on judicial conflicts of interest and the open sale of illegal drugs in Cook County Forest Preserves.
He was also a finalist the Lisagor Award for Editorial Cartoons.
He was the news editor for the Sun-Times' afternoon edition, the Chicago Sun-Times Final Markets and the Chicago Sun-Times PM edition.
He also co-authored ``Victims of Justice'' and contributed, and edited ``20th Century Chicago: 100 Years,100 Voices,'' a chronological history.
