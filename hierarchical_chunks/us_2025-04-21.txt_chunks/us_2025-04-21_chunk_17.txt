Mr. Salvatore's career in the restaurant industry began at a young age, working alongside his father, Joseph.
After nearly four decades in the business, he took his passion to new heights by opening Russell's Steaks, Chops, & More at the age of 75, proving that his dedication to the industry remains as strong as ever.
Throughout his career, 
Mr. Salvatore has made significant investments within Western New York, including funding transportation vans for veterans, contributing to the rebuilding of a Boys and Girls Club after a devastating fire, supporting Shea's Performing Arts Center, and assisting with the construction of the Niagara University Russell J. Salvatore Student Commons.
His impact is also felt at the Miracle League of Grand Island's special needs baseball diamond, and he has supported countless educational and healthcare organizations across the region.
In addition to his remarkable contributions, 
Mr. Salvatore's commitment to giving back has been further recognized through numerous accolades, including being named Humanitarian of the Year in 2018.
His foundation continues to support organizations like ECMC, Kaleida Health, Oishei Children's Hospital, Catholic Health, and other organizations throughout Western New York.
