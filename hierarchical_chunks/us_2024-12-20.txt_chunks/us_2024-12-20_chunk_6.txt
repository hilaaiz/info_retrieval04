Mr. EDWARDS.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor 
Mr. Dawson Connell, for his work as an intern serving in my D.C. office.
I would like to take this opportunity to thank him for his hard work and recognize the meaningful contributions he has made to the office this fall.
Dawson is from Waxhaw, North Carolina, and is currently a getting his Master's in Political Science at American University.
Dawson is a graduate of Mars Hill University in western North Carolina and majored in History with both Pre-Law Studies and Political Science minors.
Dawson was on the varsity baseball team at Mars Hill and is an impressive golfer.
Interns work with staff to serve constituents in North Carolina's 11th congressional district.
Dawson was chosen from a competitive pool and impressive work ethic.
It was a pleasure to work with Dawson, and I am so proud to have such an intelligent and ambitious intern representing my office.
Dawson exemplified the spirit of public service and represents the best that our Nation has to offer.
I congratulate Dawson on this successful completion of his internship, and thank him on behalf of the people of Western North Carolina.
