Ms. WILSON.
Mr. Speaker, I rise to honor the outstanding service of Genesis Santiago, who has made an exceptional contribution to my office during her time as a Congressional Hispanic Caucus Institute Fellow.
Genesis has served with distinction in my office and dedicated her time to serving the people of Florida's 24th Congressional District.
Genesis holds an impressive academic background, earning three undergraduate degrees from the University of South Florida in History, Secondary Social Studies Education, and Classics and a master's degree in public policy from Georgetown University.
Genesis demonstrated exceptional leadership and a deep commitment to advancing education equity during her fellowship.
Her extensive experience as a teacher brought a wealth of knowledge that greatly enhanced our education policy efforts.
Genesis immersed herself in the legislative process, consistently delivering high-quality briefing memos and policy recommendations.
In addition to her policy work in higher education, Genesis played a key role in drafting bills and collaborating with several organizations to move our legislative priorities forward.
Her ability to articulate complex ideas through Op-eds and legislative writing further underscored her dedication to the issues we care deeply about.
