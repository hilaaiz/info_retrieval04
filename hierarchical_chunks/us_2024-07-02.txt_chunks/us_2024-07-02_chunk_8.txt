Mr. BOYLE.
Mr. Speaker, I rise to commemorate the 250th Anniversary of the First Continental Congress and Young People's Continental Congress.
The United States Congress traces its roots to the First Continental Congress, which convened in Carpenters' Hall in 1774 and was one of the most significant events in the founding of our Nation.
Colonial delegates solidified a united American identity by adopting the Declaration of Colonial Rights, which outlined their grievances with Great Britain, and the Articles of Association, which created the colonial coalition that later signed the Declaration of Independence and fought the American Revolution.
