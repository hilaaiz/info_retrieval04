Ms. NORTON.
Mr. Speaker, today, I introduce the Increased Transparency in 501(c)(4)
Organizations Act of 2024.
This bill would require the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to make publicly available the forms organizations that self-declare under Section 501(c)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code (IRC) file with the IRS.
Americans have the right to know which organizations are operating under this section of the IRC.
To be eligible for tax-exempt status under 501(c)(4), organizations, often referred to as ``social welfare organizations,'' must be ``devoted exclusively to charitable, educational, or recreational purposes.''
They can apply for 501(c)(4) status, or they can selfdeclare.
Previously, organizations seeking to self-declare were not required to notify the IRS of their existence.
In 2015, however, the Protecting Americans from Tax Hikes Act of 2015 (PATH Act), which required an organization seeking to self-declare to file a notice with the IRS, was enacted into law.
The PATH Act did not, however, make the filed notices, Form 8976, subject to public disclosure The IRS has opined that Form 8976 cannot be made available under the Freedom of Information Act or other disclosure laws.
