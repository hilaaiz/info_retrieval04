Mr. Yera's distinguished career began in 1973 when he enlisted in the United States Army, marking the start of his lifelong commitment to federal service.
After serving on active duty, he joined the United States Army Reserve in 1986.
Following his military service, 
Mr. Yera began a career in immigration enforcement.
In 1999, he started as a Border Patrol Agent with the Department of Justice and the former Immigration and Naturalization Service.
His exemplary service led him to roles as an adjudicator and investigator.
With the formation of the Department of Homeland Security in 2003, 
Mr. Yera became the Special Agent in Charge of the Buffalo Field Office, a position he held until 2012.
Since 2013, 
Mr. Yera has served as an Immigration Officer in the Fraud Detection and National Security Directorate at the Buffalo Field Office of United States Citizenship and Immigration Services.
In this role, he has been instrumental in maintaining the integrity of our immigration system and the safety of our nation.
Please join me in congratulating 
Mr. Yera on his retirement and expressing our profound gratitude for over 50 years of unwavering dedication to our community and Nation.
