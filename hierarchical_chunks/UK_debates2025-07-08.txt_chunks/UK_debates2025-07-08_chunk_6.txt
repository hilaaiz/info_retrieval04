The Liberal Prime Minister William Gladstone was right when he said, “Justice delayed is justice denied”.
He would look on the inheritance that this Government received from the Conservative Government as a matter for great shame.
While creative solutions are required to   tackle the backlog, the jury trial—which we hear may be at risk for some—is a critical safeguard on state power, and is key to a liberal and free society.
Ahead of the Leveson report, which is coming out very soon, can the Government tell us how they will increase the overall capacity of the courts system to dispense justice, as opposed to potentially undermining justice altogether?
