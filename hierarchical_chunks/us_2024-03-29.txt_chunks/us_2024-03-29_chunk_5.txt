Mr. PAPPAS.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today in recognition of Diane Fitzpatrick, Chief Executive Officer of the Boys & Girls Club of Manchester, for being named Executive of the Year by the Nation's over 1000 clubs.
This award is determined by the Professional Association of Boys & Girls Clubs and by Diane's peers throughout the country.
As the Executive of the Year, Diane has been recognized for her significant impact on the Manchester community and the youth development profession at a local, regional, and national level.
As CEO of the Boys & Girls Club of Manchester, Diane works with staff, the board of trustees, and community members to serve children and families across the city.
With over 30 years of experience in higher education, marketing, and business development, Diane has pursued a bold and innovative vision for the Boys & Girls Club since she joined the organization in 2014.
In this role, Diane oversees strategic planning, fundraising, community relations, and partnership development.
She is widely regarded by staff as an indispensable leader and among the broader New Hampshire community as critical to the advancement of children's issues.
I commend Diane for winning this prestigious award and will continue to support her work to help youth reach their full potential.
I look forward to seeing what Diane will accomplish for the Manchester community in the years to come, and I am confident she will continue to uplift kids and families in our district.
On behalf of all the constituents of New Hampshire's First Congressional District, I applaud Diane for her commendable leadership, and wish her success in future endeavors.
