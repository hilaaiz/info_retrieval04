Earlier today, before the House was sitting, I was on a call about constituents who had lost money in an investment and are in a desperate situation.
In that case, it was because of criminal activity by a fraudster.
Their life savings have gone.
The people who have invested and bought those shares, often very humble families who have worked hard all their lives, need some compensation.
A student debating society might be tempted to say, “Let’s take it all back, and forget about the impact,” but we cannot forget about the impact, because it often falls on low-paid, hard-working people who are taxpayers too—they would end up paying a double whammy.
