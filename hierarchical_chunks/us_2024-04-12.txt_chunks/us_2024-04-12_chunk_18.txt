Ms. Stone's career with the DoD started in 1989 with Army Medicine in Europe where she served for 10 years and played a pivotal role in implementing centralized medical business operations across Army Europe, aligning personnel, workload, and financial systems.
In 1999, she was named the Army Medical Expense and Reporting Program Manager.
As Program Manager, she provided crucial medical cost accounting education and training to Army military treatment facilities, significantly impacting the Medical Expense and Performance Reporting System.
In 2004, 
Ms. Stone spearheaded the development of the U.S. Army Medical Command (MEDCOM) Performance Planning Program, translating strategy into action and facilitating the budget allocation of $6.3 billion for healthcare delivery.
In February 2018, 
Ms. Stone received recognition from the Chief of Staff of the U.S. Army for her contributions to MEDCOM and the Army.
Finally, in December 2018, 
Ms. Stone was requested to lead, develop, and implement the Defense Health Agency's Quadruple Aim Performance Planning process.
Since then, 
Ms. Stone created a single, integrated, transparent process to align military treatment facilities with the Defense Health Agency's priorities.
Ms Stone received the DoD Spirit of Service Award for her display of honor, integrity, and excellence through outstanding support as a public servant to the U.S. Department Throughout her career, 
Ms. Stone has trained over 30,000 personnel across the Military Health System.
Her commitment to service and her passion for sharing her knowledge and skills have proven to be valuable assets to her team.
Her contributions have had a profound and positive impact on the Military Health System.
