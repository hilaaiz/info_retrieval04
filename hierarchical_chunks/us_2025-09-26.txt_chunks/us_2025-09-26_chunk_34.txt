Mr. Arroyo chose to serve as a public defender, representing the poor and ensuring the constitutional rights of the most vulnerable were protected.
In 2022, he transitioned into private practice, co-founding the Arroyo Law Firm in Orlando, Florida, with his wife and law partner, Josephine Arroyo.
Under their leadership, the firm has become one of Central Florida's most respected criminal defense and immigration practices, recognized for zealous advocacy, service to underserved communities, and its particular commitment to assisting the Hispanic community.
In addition, 
Mr. Arroyo is a frequent commentator in both national and local media on issues of criminal justice, immigration, and civil rights.
While many in the legal community have remained silent, he has consistently spoken up in defense of our Constitution and immigrant communities during these dark times.
His journey--from Puerto Rico to the United Nations, to the White House, and now to the courtroom--stands as a testament to perseverance, courage, and the enduring power of advocacy in the pursuit of justice for all.
