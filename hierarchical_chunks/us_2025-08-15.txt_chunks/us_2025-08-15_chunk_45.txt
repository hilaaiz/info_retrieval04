Mr. Parker dedicated 27 years to sharing his musical expertise and passion with QCSD students.
From 1998 to 2004, he directed the awardwinning Milford Middle School band, even earning them a performance at the 2003 Pennsylvania Music Educators Association.
During his six-year tenure, the Milford Jazz Band received nothing less than a superior rating at 26 consecutive jazz festivals--an extraordinary feat.
For the past 21 years, 
Mr. Parker has served as the Director of Bands at Quakertown High School, where he led the Symphonic Band, Wind Symphony, Jazz Ensemble, and numerous other small ensembles.
Under his direction, these ensembles were invited to perform at the PMEA Conference, the NAfME National Conference, and the NAfME Eastern Division Conference an impressive fourteen times.
Additionally, 
Mr. Parker directed Quakertown High School's awardwinning marching band, which has won both national and state-level competitions under his leadership.
At this year's PMEA Conference, 
Mr. Parker was honored as the Phi Beta Mu Outstanding PA Bandmaster.
Over the years, many of his students have been selected to participate in PMEA district, region, and state band festivals.
Outside of his work at QCSD, 
Mr. Parker has taught saxophone lessons for the past 38 years.
He is an active clinician, adjudicator, and guest conductor, having led bands across Pennsylvania and Vermont.
He is a dedicated member of PMEA, NAfME, QCEA, PSEA, NEA, and Phi Beta Mu.
As a performer, 
Mr. Parker has experience as a saxophonist with wind bands, big bands, and variety bands in the Lehigh Valley and Philadelphia areas, and has performed internationally in Great Britain and the British Virgin islands.
In retirement, 
Mr. Parker looks forward to guest conducting, running clinics and teaching lessons online, as well as continuing to play his saxophone.
He is the proud father of one daughter.
