The center collaborated with NASA on missions such as Swift, NuSTAR, and Fermi, producing educator guides, simulations, and videos used nationwide.
EdEon also created programming for the NSF's LIGO project and led teacher training workshops that strengthened STEM education in underserved communities.
Among her many honors, Dr. Cominsky is the recipient of the Frank J. Malina Astronautics Medal, the American Astronomical Society Education Prize, the Sally Ride Excellence in Education Award, and was elected a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement.
Her work has been recognized nationally for advancing science education and outreach.
Dr. Cominsky currently resides in Sonoma County.
She was married to the late Garret Jernigan, with whom she shared 42 years of partnership until his passing in 2022.
In addition to her professional work, she is a dedicated mentor, public speaker, and advocate for expanding access to STEM fields across communities.
