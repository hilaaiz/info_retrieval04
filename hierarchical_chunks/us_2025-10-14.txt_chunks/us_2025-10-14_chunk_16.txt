Based in Washington, D.C., she directs a team responsible for government contracting, commercial sales, marketing, customer success, and strategic partnerships, ensuring reliable power solutions in mission-critical environments.
Originally from Canada, Caltha has been with Eaton for nearly a decade, serving in many leadership roles spanning new product development, product marketing, and channel management.
She is a graduate of Eaton's Global Leadership Development Program and previously oversaw $262 million in distribution partner sales as the company's first National Channel Manager for Industrial Controls.
Her leadership extends far beyond business into nonprofit, cultural, and faith-based service.
As co-founder and chair of Heaven to the Yeah, she empowers women in sports, championing the women's decathlon for the 2032 Olympics in Brisbane while sponsoring athletes internationally.
She is a board member of Global Hope, serving vulnerable populations in Iraq and the Middle East by developing sustainable solutions for women and children.
She also serves as corporate chair for Influence Women, chairs the Salvation Army Western Territory Advisory Board, contributes to Women Business Collaborative's in the Arena initiative, and advises the Cleveland Orchestra.
Guided by her deep faith in Jesus Christ, Caltha sees her work as a calling to serve with integrity, compassion, and excellence.
A member of She Leads America, she is being honored in 2025 as a Christian Woman of Distinction, exemplifying faith in action across every sphere of influence.
