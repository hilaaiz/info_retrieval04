Ms. TOKUDA.
Mr. Speaker, Hawaii's constitution recognizes two official languages: English and Hawaiian.
Each February, we celebrate Mahina `Olelo Hawai`i, or Hawaiian Language Month.
It's time for us to honor and promote the use of Hawaiian language, and to achieve this, we must create opportunities and environments where it can be celebrated and utilized.
`Olelo Hawai`i is more than a language, it is a connection to a history and a culture that must never be forgotten.
That is why it was so important to me to speak it today on the House floor.
Long live the Hawaiian language.
