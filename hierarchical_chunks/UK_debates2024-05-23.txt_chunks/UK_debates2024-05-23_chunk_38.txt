The Minister is always compassionate and understands the issues, as we can tell from the way she responds.
As I often say, however, the benefits system leads to incredible frustration.
When those who are disabled have their applications refused, they go to appeal.
The biggest issue in my office is benefits, including appeals; we have one staff member who does nothing else during the five-and-a-half-day week that she works.
Although we recognise that DWP staff do a good job, there needs to be a better understanding of how the system works.
When someone fills in their application, there needs to be a better understanding of what it means to have chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, osteoarthritis, diabetes, blood pressure problems, back pain, chronic pain.
Those are the issues.
When we win 85% of appeals, it indicates that perhaps the first decision was not right.
