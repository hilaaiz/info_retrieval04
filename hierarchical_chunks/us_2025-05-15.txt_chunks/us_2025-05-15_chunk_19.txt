There, she not only became a devout Christian but also an ordained Evangelist, embodying the strength and devotion of her faith.
Despite never obtaining a driver's license, Johnnie Mae remained a steadfast, lifelong rider of the Chicago Transit Authority, traveling daily by bus for her work and church duties.
Her determination and resilience were reflected in every aspect of her life.
Johnnie Mae was revered by all who knew her.
In the neighborhood, when she walked down the street, there was an unspoken respect.
Neighbors would remove their hats, hide their beer cans, and straighten up their behavior, for they knew that ``Momma Streater'' was a woman of integrity, a true representative of God's love.
Her presence commanded respect, and no one wanted to disappoint her with anything less than their best.
Johnnie Mae was the rock of her family, the heart of her community, and a source of unwavering love and strength.
Her absence will be deeply felt by all who had the privilege of knowing her.
Mr. Speaker, I ask that my colleagues join me in honoring the life.
