Mr. JACKSON.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor the remarkable life of Johnnie Mae Streater, a woman who graced this world for 81 years.
While her passing marks the end of an era, her legacy of selfless service to others and her unwavering faith will continue to live on through the family she leaves behind, her beloved daughter, Tamond, five grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.
Born on October 28, 1942, in Leesburg, Florida, Johnnie Mae was the daughter of Annie Lee Davis, a homemaker, and Samuel Streater, a steelworker.
She grew up with a strong foundation of love, faith, and hard work that would guide her throughout her life.
``Momma-Streater,'' as she was known by her family, graduated from Fifth Avenue High School in Pittsburg, Pennsylvania, in 1962.
Soon after, she relocated to Illinois, where she began her dedicated career as a Caseworker with the Illinois Department.
For 30 years, she served the community with compassion and integrity, touching countless lives before retiring with grace.
In the 1970's, Johnnie Mae embraced the Lord Jesus Christ into her life, a decision that would profoundly shape the rest of her life.
