Ms. Quinn's contributions to healthcare and labor advocacy are profound.
A proud graduate of SUNY Erie, Ms Quinn was part of its first graduating class of Registered Nurses--a landmark moment in the college's history.
For nearly four decades, she has served with excellence at Kenmore Mercy Hospital, where her clinical expertise, care and professionalism have earned the respect of colleagues and recognition from countless patients.
Her influence extends well beyond the hospital, as vice president of Communications Workers of America Local 1199 and a dedicated member of the New York State Safe Staffing Committee; she has been a tireless advocate for safe patient care, equitable working conditions, and the rights of healthcare workers across the state.
Her leadership was essential during the CWA strike at Mercy Hospital, where she fought for union members' rights and engaged directly with local, state, and federal elected officials.
Thanks to her leadership, the hardworking employees at Mercy earned a hard-won contract with improved compensation and safer working conditions, benefiting patients and workers alike.
In addition to her advocacy, 
Ms. Quinn is a beloved member of her local community and Episcopal church where she is spiritually guided by Father Curry and Bishop Michael Curly.
Her unwavering dedication to helping other is reflected in her generous support providing food, housing, clothing, and transportation to those in need.
