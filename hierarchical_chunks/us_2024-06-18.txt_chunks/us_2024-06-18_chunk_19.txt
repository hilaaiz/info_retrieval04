Mr. DeSAULNIER.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize Eric Volta as he retires from his role as Superintendent of the Liberty Union High School District.
Eric grew up in the Santa Clara Valley and graduated from Santa Clara High School.
He went on to attend Stanford University, playing on Stanford's football team all four years of university and graduating with his BA in history.
Throughout his career, Eric has worked tirelessly to support students and expand educational opportunities throughout the Bay Area.
He worked in the Santa Clara Unified School District (SCUSD) teaching 9th through 12th graders and went on to serve as Vice Principal.
Eric and his family moved to Brentwood, California in 2000 and Eric joined the Liberty Union High School District (LUHSD).
During his time with LUHSD, Eric served as assistant principal, high school principal, and Assistant Superintendent.
He was promoted to Superintendent in 2011 where he has worked to support students and families.
Eric has received a number of accolades for his work as an educator, receiving the ACSA's Superintendent of the Year for Region VI Award, the California Interscholastic Federation Distinguished Services Award, and the California Department of Education's Principal Award.
Additionally, he served on the California School Boards Association Superintendents Advisory Council and is a current board member of the California High School Coalition.
Eric is retiring from his position as superintendent after 13 years contributions to the Liberty Union High School District and wishing him well as he begins his new role as superintendent of the Mountain View Los Altos Union High School District.
