Mr. SOTO.
Mr. Speaker, Lenna Donna Cadogan hails from Guyana, South America.
Lenna received an academic scholarship from the government of Guyana to attend the Dental Auxiliary School in Jamaica, where she pursued advanced studies and graduated as a dental nurse.
In 1980, she migrated to the United States and resided in Brooklyn, New York.
She continued her studies at New York City Technical College, where she graduated as a dental hygienist.
In 1988, she relocated with her family to Orlando, Florida, and was employed as a dental hygienist.
She is currently retired after 30 years of service.
Lenna has been married to her husband, Noel, for over 46 years and has two amazing children and four grandchildren, as well as a son-in-law and daughter-in-law.
Lenna has been a member of the Caribbean and Floridian Association, Inc., since its inception 34 years ago.
She has been a member of the Medical Committee serving the community in health fairs, and the Education Committee, which provides scholarships yearly to graduating students in Central Florida.
She is currently the chairperson of the Fundraising and Entertainment Committee, a position she has held for many years.
She is instrumental in coordinating one of the largest festivals, the Caribbean Fusion Festival, which is held annually in Kissimmee and attended by over 7,000 people who contribute greatly to the economy of the City.
She also organizes yearly bus rides to Fort DeSota Park, as well as the annual holiday party at Christmas for family, friends, and members of the community.
Lenna is a woman of faith and is an active member of Solid Rock Community Church in Kissimmee.
She is also the leader of the Greeters Ministry.
