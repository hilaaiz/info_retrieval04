Humanitarian aid should never be contingent on a ceasefire or used as a political tool.
On  28 January , the then Minister for development, my right hon.
Friend  the Member for Oxford East (Anneliese Dodds) , announced £17 million in funding to ensure that healthcare, food and shelter could reach tens of thousands of civilians, and to support vital infrastructure across the Occupied Palestinian Territories.
The UK has announced £129 million of funding for the OPTs so far this financial year, including £41 million for the United Nations Relief and Works Agency.
The hon.
Member for North Herefordshire (Ellie Chowns)  asked about the long-term ceasefire prospects.
The UK plays its part in pushing both sides towards a hopeful future for the region.
We are working with not just Arab states, but partners such as the US to try to push for a solution that is in line with international humanitarian law.
