Mr. SOTO.
Mr. Speaker, Priya Persaud serves as the founder and director of the Devyani School.
Her journey in dance commenced in 1992, during which she received training in Odissi dance at the Chitralekha Dance Academy and Bharatanatyam at the Sampradaya Dance Academy.
In 2010, Priya committed to educating others about Indian dance and culture, integrating lessons on various Indian festivals, Hidni, and the profound significance of the postures and gestures in Indian dance.
Her foremost passion lies in transmitting the rich Indian cultural heritage through the art of dance, and she takes great pleasure in witnessing her students and the community engage with and learn about Indian traditions.
Over the past 15 years, the dancers from the Devyani School of Cultural Arts have showcased their talents across Orlando and Kissimmee, performing at venues such as Walt Disney World, Silver Spurs Arena, Kissimmee Lakefront Park, Boggy Creek Elementary School, and the Osceola Sheriff's Department.
Priya has also been involved with various organizations that advocate for diversity and cultural acceptance, serving as a Steering Committee member for FusionFest, currently holding a board position with AsiaTrend, and collaborating with numerous local organizations in Central Florida, including the Indian American Business Associations and Chamber, Indian American Chamber of Commerce, TEMA, and the Association.
Cultural appreciation and education have consistently been a priority for Priya.
She strives to bridge the divide between ignorance and acceptance regarding diversity.
With 20 years of experience as a public sector employee, Priya takes pleasure in serving her community in various capacities.
She aspires for her impact on the community and future generations to be beneficial, fostering an environment where diversity and cultural acceptance are standard practices.
