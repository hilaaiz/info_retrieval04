Mr. SOTO.
Mr. Speaker Pro Tempore, Iris Bathsheba Rueda Martinez, a Mexican attorney, graduated from Universidad del Tepeyac, Mexico City, stands as a prominent figure within the Central Florida Hispanic community.
Her illustrious career spans a remarkable 17-year tenure of service at the Consulates of Mexico in Orlando, Florida, and Fresno, California.
Iris has dedicated her life to providing unwavering support and making a profound impact on the lives of her fellow Mexican compatriots living abroad while working closely with community organizations, legal experts, and government officials to address the unique needs of Mexican nationals living in the United States.
Born in the vibrant heart of Mexico City, Iris has ardently maintained a deep connection to her Hispanic cultural heritage throughout her life journey.
Her commitment to the promotion and celebration of Hispanic heritage resonates in every facet of her existence, with deep gratitude for the guiding influence of her faith in God.
Throughout her distinguished consular career, Iris played a determinate and indispensable role in delivering humanitarian, community, and legal assistance to Mexican citizens residing in Florida and California.
She participated in projects that led to the protection the success of mutual international relations based on the power of diplomacy.
Beyond her consular duties and advocacy work, Iris takes immense pride in her roles as a devoted wife and mother to a cherished daughter.
The bedrock of family values holds central importance in her life, with her family in Mexico serving as an unwavering pillar of support.
One of Iris's deepest aspirations is to instill in her daughter an enduring love for her Mexican heritage and a profound sense Currently, Iris lends her expertise and dedication to a distinguished international consultancy firm Lord and Lord Consulting, led by accomplished women.
She plays a key role in the management of the relations between LATAM, the USA, and the communication with the U.S. federal government.
The selection of Iris as an honoree during Hispanic Heritage Month is a well-deserved recognition of her dedication and contribution to the promotion of the Hispanic community in the United States.
