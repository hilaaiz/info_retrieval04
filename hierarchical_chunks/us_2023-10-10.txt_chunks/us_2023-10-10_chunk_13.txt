Mr. GARAMENDI.
Mr. Speaker pro tempore, I rise today to recognize the contributions of Courtney Tindall as she is honored as one of California's 8th Congressional District's Women of the Year.
Courtney serves as a Senior Dependency Drug Court Case Manager for the Solano County Superior Court.
In this role, she advocates for those who suffer from substance use disorder and has graduated hundreds of parents who have regained custody of their children.
She is committed to helping those struggling with substance use disorder become sober and better their lives.
Every day, she displays compassion and empathy as she advocates for children and families.
Courtney has an unfailing commitment to her work and to reuniting families.
She has received numerous awards for her work, including the Superior Court of California Customer Service Award, the California Bar Association Court Staff Award, the Child Abuse Prevention Council Henry Bergh Award for Practitioner of the Year, and Henry Bergh Award for Outstanding Contributions in the Dependency Drug Court and Support of Child Abuse Prevention, Intervention, and Treatment Efforts, The work Courtney does has touched the lives of countless vulnerable individuals and helped them make change for the better.
On behalf of the people of California's 8th Congressional District, it is my pleasure to honor and recognize Courtney Tindall for her positive contributions to our community.
