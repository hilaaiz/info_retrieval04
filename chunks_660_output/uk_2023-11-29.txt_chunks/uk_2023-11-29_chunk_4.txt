Bill retired from education in 2013.
He taught school at Louisville

Municipal School District and made a difference in the lives of many

students throughout his teaching career.
Serving as a principal at the

Kosciusko Public School, Bill led the school and eventually the school

district as superintendent of education.
He also worked in the Union

Public School District and the Polk County School District in Tennessee

as superintendent of education.
Bill's legacy in education continued after his retirement when he co-

founded Performance Based Education Company, LLC, which helps students

maintain achievement progress by adding capacity and working with

school districts to improve their student achievement.
Bill worked as

the vice president, contract manager, marketing specialist, and program

developer.
Mr. Speaker, I am thankful for the opportunity to memorialize the

life and legacy of William Wade.
Bill has made an impact on the lives

of students throughout Mississippi and Tennessee.
His impact will be

felt for generations to come.
His loss will be felt by all those who

have been under his leadership.
