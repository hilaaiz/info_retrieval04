HONORING LISA NELSON-BONNINGTON AS ONE OF CALIFORNIA'S 8TH

CONGRESSIONAL DISTRICT'S WOMEN OF THE YEAR

_____

HON.
JOHN GARAMENDI

Mr. GARAMENDI.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor Lisa Nelson-

Bonnington, recognized as one of California's 8th Congressional

District's Women of the Year.
In addition to being a prominent business

leader and community advocate, Lisa currently serves as the President

and Chief Executive Officer of the Fairfield-Suisun Chamber of

Commerce.
With more than thirty years of experience in marketing,

public relations, and organizational management, Lisa has dedicated her

career to strengthening local economies and supporting small businesses

across the Bay Area.
Prior to her current role, she served as President

and CEO of the Martinez Chamber of Commerce and as Vice President of

the Greater Concord Chamber of Commerce, where she championed

initiatives to promote economic growth and workforce development.
As an

active participant in the Western Association of Chamber Executives

Academy, Lisa brings both expertise and passion to her work fostering

collaboration between businesses, government, and community

organizations.
Since joining the Fairfield-Suisun Chamber in 2024, she

has focused on expanding outreach to local entrepreneurs and

strengthening partnerships that enhance economic vitality for both

Fairfield and Suisun City.
Lisa's work is rooted in inclusion and long-

term sustainability, ensuring that every sector of the community has a

voice in shaping its shared prosperity.
Through her service, Lisa

continues to embody integrity, innovation, and a steadfast commitment

to empowering others.
On behalf of the people of California's 8th

Congressional District, it is my pleasure to honor and recognize Lisa

Nelson-Bonnington for her outstanding leadership and unwavering

dedication to economic and community development.

HONORING LUCINDA BAZILE AS ONE OF CALIFORNIA'S 8TH CONGRESSIONAL

DISTRICT'S WOMEN OF THE YEAR

_____

HON.
JOHN GARAMENDI

Mr. GARAMENDI.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor Lucinda Bazile,

recognized as one of California's 8th Congressional District's Women of

the Year.
As Chief Executive Officer of Cancer Support Community San

Francisco Bay Area, Lucinda has dedicated her life to advancing health

equity, expanding access to care, and empowering those affected by

cancer and chronic illness.
With over three decades of experience in

community health and nonprofit leadership, she has built a legacy

defined by compassion, integrity, and impact.
Prior to leading Cancer

Support Community, Lucinda served as Deputy Director at Lifelong

Medical Care, where she helped establish key departments, including

Human Resources, Diversity, Equity & Inclusion, AmeriCorps, Outreach,

Compliance, and Marketing.
Her leadership strengthened partnerships

among healthcare providers, community organizations, and public

institutions, ensuring that underserved populations, especially seniors

and communities of color, receive the care and dignity they deserve.
Deeply motivated by personal experience, Lucinda transformed loss into

purpose, becoming a tireless advocate for early diagnosis, patient

education, and culturally competent care.
A proud member of Delta Sigma

Theta Sorority, Inc., she mentors young women, broadens pathways into

healthcare careers, and continues to champion diversity and inclusion

in medicine.
Her advocacy extends beyond professional settings as she

remains an active volunteer supporting seniors and families throughout

the Bay Area.
Through her unwavering commitment to health justice and

community empowerment, Lucinda Bazile exemplifies what it means to lead

with purpose and compassion.
On behalf of the people of California's

8th Congressional District, it is my honor to recognize Lucinda Bazile

for her invaluable contributions to our community.
