Mr. Speaker, it is my honor today to recognize Richard A. Duerr, Sr.,
a Naval veteran and firefighter, for his many years of service to our community.
Richard began his career in the United States Navy, serving our country as a Communications Yeoman 2nd Class from 1965 to 1969.
Following his military service, Richard embarked on careers in the private sector, working for more than 16 years at Revere Copper and Brass in Rome, NY, and also at Sanford Fire Equipment and Turning Stone Resort Casino.
Beyond his professional endeavors, Richard has engaged in many years of service to his community.
For 51 years, he has been an integral member of the Camden Volunteer Fire Department, holding vital roles such as Assistant Chief and Fire Police Captain, a position he held for 26 years.
Richard is a Life Member of the Volunteer Fire Police Association of the State of New York (VFPASNY), currently serving as president.
He was also Chairman of the Seminar Committee for 15 years, Editor of the VFPASNY Newspaper and a trainer in multiple counties for the Fire Police Review Class for 12 years.
Richard is a Life Member of the Oneida County Volunteer Fire Police Association where he served as President for 12 years and has held various other positions including the role of Chaplain.
Additionally, Richard contributed to the work of the New York State Chaplains Association, conducting memorial and funeral services, and to the Oneida County Volunteer Firefighters Association, where Richard is currently the Chaplain and Sergeant at Arms.
In addition to these many accomplishments, Richard has been a member of the American Legion Post 66 in Camden, New York for 26 years, and is a member of the Firefighters Association of the State of New York (FASNY).
Richard has received various awards including the George Woodman Award for Excellence, seven President's Awards for Community and Fire Police Leadership, and the VFPASNY Fire Police Officer of the Year.
These, along with several other certificates and plaques reflect Richard's many contributions.
On behalf of New York's 22nd District, I am pleased to recognize the service and accomplishments of Richard Duerr, Sr.
I thank him for his dedication to our community and our country. ____________________

Mr. Speaker, I rise today to recognize and congratulate Mr. Scott Gray as he is awarded the Airport Professional of the Year Award by the Texas Department of Transportation.
This prestigious accolade is a testament to Mr. Gray's exemplary leadership, dedication, and contributions to the aviation industry.
During his tenure as the airport manager of Denton Enterprise Airport from 2017 to 2023, Mr. Gray demonstrated exceptional proficiency in managing the day-to-day operations, planning, and development of one of Texas' busiest airports.
Under his guidance, Denton Enterprise Airport grew significantly, rising from 7th busiest airport in Texas as he arrived, to become the 5th busiest airport in Texas and among the top 60 nationwide, with over 212,000 operations in 2023.
Mr. Gray's strategic vision and strong analytical skills have been instrumental in the airport's growth and success.
He oversaw significant milestones, including the delivery of the airport's second runway in 2019 and the expansion of the main runway in 2021.
