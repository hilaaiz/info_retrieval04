She departed the Navy in

1997.
During her service she earned the National Defense Service Medal,

Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal, Sea Service Medal Deployment Ribbon

three times, and the Good Conduct Medal two times.
Following her military career, she earned a bachelor's degree in

social work with honors from Texas Tech University.
She went on to

serve in several public service roles in Waco, Texas, including at the

Texas Youth Commission, Texas Behavioral Health Network, and Child

Protective Services.
Since 2007, she has worked at the Waco VA Medical

Center as a Mental Health Caseworker for seriously mentally ill women

Veterans.
Beyond her professional work, Nola leads numerous Veteran-focused

programs as Commander of VFW Post 2148 and volunteers in the community.
She supports troop care packages, family assistance, welcome-home

events, Veteran services, youth scholarships, and JROTC programs.
She

is also a member of the Honor Guard and an adult volunteer with the

Waco Young Marines.
Recently, she made history as the first Native American female

Commander of VFW District 29, overseeing seven posts in Texas.
Nola is

also a proud mother of four successful children, all of whom have

pursued higher education or military service.
Nola Saldana exemplifies

lifelong service, leadership, and dedication to others.
It is with

great honor and respect that I present Nola Jane Saldana with a

Congressional Veteran Commendation from the 17th Congressional District

of Texas.
