But his heart was rooted in Dorado, and he devoted himself to serving

the very place that had shaped him.
That calling led him to the mayor's office, where on August 3, 1987,

he was sworn in as the mayor of Dorado.
What followed was nothing short

of historic.
For 38 years, through 10 consecutive elections, Carlitos

was entrusted by his neighbors with the responsibility of guiding their

community.
He transformed Dorado into a thriving center of tourism and

development while never losing sight of the needs of working families.
He often described Dorado as the ``paradise of Puerto Rico, where life

is beautiful,'' and under his leadership, that vision became a reality.
But Carlitos was more than just an astute and skillful politician.
He

was a man of loyalty, wit, and heart.
He had a gift for words, painting

images and phrases that stuck in the memory of the people he served.
He

balanced economic progress with social justice, reminding all who knew

him that growth should never leave anyone behind.
And to those who knew

him best, he will always be remembered for his loyalty.
In the hardest

and loneliest moments, Carlitos was there, steadfast and encouraging,

urging us forward ``at full speed and with even greater strength.''
Carlitos made history as one of the longest-serving mayors in Puerto

Rican history, second only to his contemporary in Manati, Juan Aubin

Cruz Manzano.
Yet beyond the records and accomplishments, his true

legacy will live on in the people of Dorado, who saw in him not only a

mayor, but a neighbor, a motivator, and a guardian of their community's

spirit.
Mr. Speaker, the life of Carlos Alberto `Carlitos' Lopez Rivera

reminds us that true leadership is measured not only in years of

service, but in the lives uplifted along the way.
Our prayers are with

his family, and I respectfully ask this body to rise in tribute to his

life and service.
As we reflect on his legacy, may we honor him by

carrying his spirit forward, always pressing on ``at full speed and

with even greater strength.''
The following text is a direct Spanish translation of the statement

above:

Mr. Speaker, me levanto hoy para rendir homenaje a la

memoria y el legado de un servidor publico extraordinario,

Carlos Alberto Lopez Rivera, conocido por su querido pueblo

como ``Carlitos.''
Nacido el 14 de septiembre de 1958 en Dorado, Carlitos

crecio con los valores de trabajo duro, servicio y lealtad

inculcados por sus padres, Pedro Lopez Maldonado y Ana

Rivera.
Tras graduarse de la Escuela Superior Jose S. Alegria

y obtener un titulo en Administracion de Empresas de la

Universidad de Puerto Rico, pudo haber elegido muchos

caminos.
Pero su corazon estaba en Dorado, y dedico su vida a

servir al pueblo que lo habia formado.
Ese llamado lo llevo a la alcaldia, donde el 3 de agosto de

1987 juro como alcalde de Dorado.
Lo que siguio fue nada

menos que historico.
Durante 38 anos, a traves de 10

elecciones consecutivas, Carlitos fue confiado por sus

vecinos con la responsabilidad de guiar a su pueblo.
Transformo a Dorado en un centro prospero de turismo y

desarrollo, sin perder nunca de vista las necesidades de las

familias trabajadoras.
A menudo describe a Dorado como el

``paraiso de Puerto Rico, donde la vida es bella,'' y bajo su

liderazgo, esa vision se convirtio en realidad.
Pero Carlitos fue mas que un politico astuto y habil.
Fue

un hombre de lealtad, ingenio y corazon.
Tenia un don para

las palabras, pintando imagenes y frases que permanecian en

la memoria de la gente a la que servia.
Supo equilibrar el

progreso economico con la justicia social, recordando a todos

los que lo conocieron que el

crecimiento no debe dejar a nadie atras.
Y para quienes lo

conocieron de cerca, siempre sera recordado por su lealtad.
