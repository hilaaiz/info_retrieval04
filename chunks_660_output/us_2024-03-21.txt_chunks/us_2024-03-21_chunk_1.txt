Mr. Speaker, Janelle Dunn is the chief executive officer of True Health, a non-profit federally qualified health center serving low-income and underserved populations within Orange and Seminole Counties.
Mrs. Dunn obtained a master's degree in health administration from the University of Central Florida and a bachelor's degree in business administration from Loyola University New Orleans.
She is a certified medical practice executive through the Medical Group Management Association, a member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., Leadership Seminole Class 32.
and a proud wife and mother.
Mrs. Dunn's tenure with True Health began in 2012 when she was a center manager.
She has held the roles of director of operations and chief operations officer before being appointed CEO amid the global pandemic in 2020.
Under Mrs. Dunn's leadership, True Health has grown from seven locations to nine, added two new pharmacies and an optometry and optical department, and acquired the Healthy Start program for Orange County to provide services for mothers and babies.
Mrs. Dunn is an advocate for access to healthcare and believes in the innovation and implementation of creative solutions to existing challenges.
As illustrated during the COVID-19 pandemic, True Health was the only FQHC in Central Florida to operate a monoclonal infusion center, which allowed patients to recover at home and avoid the hospital.
Mrs. Dunn's transformative leadership has been celebrated with prestigious recognitions, including the 2021 CEO of the Year and 2020 Diversity in Business awards from the Orlando Business Journal.
In 2023, True Health was honored as the Healthcare Business of the Year by the Seminole County Chamber of Commerce and recognized as one of the Top 100 Women-Led Organizations in Florida by The Women's Edge.
In January 2024.
True Health received the Humanitarian Award from the City of Sanford's Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Celebration Steering Committee. ____________________

Mr. Speaker, Jessica Paul is a St. Cloud resident and local business owner, who advocates for the needs of all members of her community.
As a lifelong resident of the Whitted Historic Community, the Paul roots run deep, in Osceola County.
She is a phenomenal mother to her handsome son, Jase.
Jessica works to unite voices big and small and has extensive experience connecting elected officials to pressing developmental issues facing the Whitted Historic Community, including environmental concerns that directly impact quality of life.
Jessica serves on the Osceola County CDBG Citizens Advisory Task Force for District 5, ensuring every voice gets heard.
She is also an active member of St. Luke Missionary Baptist Church of Narcoossee.
Personally, she takes an active volunteer role in coordinating local events for the Markus Paul Foundation, which honors her late cousin, a hometown hero and NFL legacy.
Jessica is passionate about making a positive impact on her community through this foundation.
Jessica and her twin sister were showcased as part of Spectrum News 13's Everyday Hero and a video spotlight series presented by Experience Kissimmee.
The feature highlighted their positive reputation for being good neighbors and contributing to the well-being of their community.
Jessica finds solace and joy by spending quality time with her supportive family.
