Date: 2023-11-15 Volume: 169, Issue: 190 Pages: E1108 - E1108 ================================================================================ [Extensions of Remarks] From the Congressional Record Online through the Government Publishing Office [ www.gpo.gov ] DAMON NELSON, STAFF DIRECTOR, HPSCI ______ of ohio in the house of representatives Mr. TURNER.
Mr. Speaker, November 10, marked the 5-year anniversary of the passing of a beloved friend and colleague, former House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence Staff Director, Damon Nelson.
Damon Nelson served his country nearly his entire life.
Before working for Rep. Devin Nunes and the people of California's 22nd Congressional District, Damon was proud to have served in the United States Air Force, where he met the love of his life and future wife, Brigadier General Dana Nelson.
He was a decorated veteran of the Persian gulf war, earning an Air Medal and three Air Achievement Medals.
Damon was known to be an immensely loyal colleague.
He worked in the office of former Intelligence Committee Chairman Devin Nunes for over 10 years.
Five years after his passing, Members and staff alike remember Damon for his hard work, boundless sense of humor, selfless commitment to mentoring others and service to the intelligence community and his country. ____________________
HONORING CRIS EDWARDS; Congressional Record Vol.
169, No.
190



Date: 2023-11-15 Volume: 169, Issue: 190 Pages: E1109 - E1109 ================================================================================ [Extensions of Remarks] From the Congressional Record Online through the Government Publishing Office [ www.gpo.gov ] HONORING CRIS EDWARDS ______ of illinois in the house of representatives Mr. BOST.
Mr. Speaker, I rise today to honor Cris Edwards of Olney, Illinois, for being named a National Distinguished Principal by the National Association of Elementary School Principals.
Cris is the principal for Richland County Elementary School, which serves nearly 1,000 rural students.
The elementary school's mission is to ``develop respectful, responsible learners and leaders.''
Under Cris Edwards' watch, that mission is being fulfilled.
Her devotion is recognized by students, parents, and teachers alike.
The school was given the high honor of ``Legacy School'' by the Leader in Me program.
They host ``High Five Fridays'' to build connections in the community.
And they launched a mobile library in an old school bus to deliver books in the summer.
Cris has earned her status as a National Distinguished Principal, just as she has earned her new position as the incoming president of the Illinois Principals Association.
Mr. Speaker, please join me in recognizing Cris Edwards for all she has done to provide a positive learning environment for Richland County schoolchildren. ____________________

Date: 2023-11-15 Volume: 169, Issue: 190 Pages: E1108 - E1109 ================================================================================ [Extensions of Remarks] [Pages E1108-E1109] From the Congressional Record Online through the Government Publishing Office [ www.gpo.gov ] HONORING LINDA KAY MITROVICH ______ of california in the house of representatives Mr. VARGAS.
Mr. Speaker, today, I rise to honor Linda Kay Mitrovich, a beloved friend, leader and mentor.
Linda was born on May 18, 1947, in Whittier.
California.
She was a dedicated public servant.
She served on the Poway City Council and was Deputy Mayor from 1986 through 1990.
She committed her life to the betterment of others and took on many important causes, including environmental protection and Native American sovereignty.
She played a significant role in creating the first Wildlife Corridor in California.
the Coal Canyon Wildlife Reserve, which supported the ecological well-being of over twenty-two million people in Southern California.
Linda also created the first national conference on Native American Sovereignty in 2000.
The California State Legislature also recognized her accomplishments by naming her Woman of the Year in 1988 and 1991.
Linda's legacy is remembered through her husband and five children, and her memory is cherished by those fortunate enough to have met her. ____________________
